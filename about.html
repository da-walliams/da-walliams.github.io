Boogie Woogie, Linguistics, & Typesetting

Who is Wally the Boogie?

Basic Information Summary
  • Name: Wally the Boogie (he/him)
  • Discipline: Linguistics
  • Research interests: Genrative Syntax
  • Languages: Japanese (native), English (advanced), Boogie Woogie (advanced), French (basic)
  • Work Experience: Part-time Editorial and Typesetting Assistant, Part-time Mathematics Tutor, English-Japanese Translator of a Textbook on Econometrics

As a Student of Linguistics

Wally the Boogie is a junior student in the Department of English Studies in the Faculty of Foreign Studies at Sophia University, Tokyo, Japan.

He is native in Japanese, advanced in English (and Boogie Woogie), and basic in French.

He majors theoretical linguistics with current interests in syntax based on Chomskyan generative linguistics.

He also has cherished his academic interest in the formal sciences, including logic, topology, formal language theory, and axiomatic Newtonian mechanics.

His aim is to integrate all of his academic backgrounds to make interdisciplinary yet canonical contributions to syntactic theory.

He currently pursues a teaching certificate for middle and high school education, exploring the practical application of his linguistic insights to ESL/EFL teaching.

As a Typesetter, Editor, and Translator

Wally the Boogie has professional experience in technologies for physically articulating the insights gained in the speculative exploration of academic branches such as the disciplines above in a standardized and accessible way, called typesetting.

He has worked as an editorial and typesetting assistant for a Japanese academic publishing company on book projects for students, educators, and researchers who have completed or are currently pursuing a college degree or higher.

He specializes in typesetting mixed-mathematics Japanese texts with LaTeX, having worked on dozens of projects, including books on statistics, physics, mathematics, programming, and econometrics.

He has been recruited to join the translation team of a forthcoming econometrics textbook, leveraging his well-rounded background in English, familiarity with mathematical expressions, and interest in the formal sciences.

He aspires to a versatile career as a professional in publishing.

As a Boogie-Woogie Lover

Wally the Boogie’s private life is that of a music lover with a keen fondness for boogie-woogie music as well as Japanese pop music.

His passion for boogie-woogie music runs so deep that he conducted a semester-long research project on American boogie-woogie music from the 1920s to the 1940s.

He admires many boogie-woogie players, including Les Frères, Keito Saito, and Axel Zwingenberger.

His primary preference lies in European boogie-woogie pianos after the boogie-woogie renaissance onward.

As an amateur boogie-woogie pianist, he has posted some of his performances on social media and has performed live on the street piano many times.

He is happy to accept any and all offers to play the boogie-woogie piano and welcomes requests and inquiries via the contact page of this website.
